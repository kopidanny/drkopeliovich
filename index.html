Treatment Focus

Dr. Kopeliovich's practice is focused exclusively on advanced implant procedures and complex reconstruction.

Full Mouth Dental Implants

Complete arch replacement for patients who have lost most or all of their teeth. This involves strategic implant placement and comprehensive prosthetic planning to restore full dental function.

All-on-4 / All-on-6

Fixed prosthetic solutions that use four to six implants to support a complete arch of teeth. These protocols can often be completed with reduced surgical time and immediate provisional teeth.

Complex Implant Reconstruction

Surgical treatment for challenging cases including failed previous implants, severe bone loss, or anatomical complications requiring specialized surgical expertise.

Bone Grafting

Procedures to rebuild jawbone structure lost due to tooth extraction, disease, or trauma. Adequate bone volume is essential for successful long-term implant outcomes.

Second Opinions

Independent evaluation of existing treatment plans or assessment of cases that have been deemed too complex. A fresh perspective from a surgical specialist can clarify options.

Why Choose an Oral &
Maxillofacial Surgeon

Dental implant placement is a surgical procedure. While many practitioners offer implant services, the complexity of your case determines what level of training is appropriate for your surgeon.

Surgical Training in Bone and Anatomy

Oral and maxillofacial surgeons complete years of hospital-based surgical residency focused specifically on the bones, nerves, and tissues of the face and jaws. This training develops a detailed understanding of the anatomical structures that must be navigated during implant surgery.

Managing Complex Cases

Advanced implant cases often involve insufficient bone, proximity to critical nerves or sinuses, or the need to coordinate multiple surgical procedures. These cases require surgical planning and execution that draws on specialized training.

Handling Complications

Surgical complications, while uncommon, do occur. A surgeon trained to manage complex anatomical situations is better prepared to address unexpected findings and minimize risk during procedures.

Reconstruction-Oriented Thinking

Rather than simply placing implants, a reconstructive approach considers the long-term function and stability of the entire jaw structure. This perspective is especially important for patients with significant bone loss or previous treatment failures.

For straightforward implant cases, many qualified practitioners can provide excellent care. For complex cases, the additional surgical training of an oral and maxillofacial surgeon can make a meaningful difference in outcomes.
